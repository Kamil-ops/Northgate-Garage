What it is

A proper diagnosis, not a guess

Modern cars have dozens of computers watching the engine, gearbox, brakes and safety systems. When something's wrong, they store a fault code — but a code on its own only points to an area, not the actual cause.

We read every module with dealer-level equipment, then confirm it with a road test and live sensor data. That's the difference between "replace the sensor and hope" and knowing exactly what needs doing — so you're not paying for parts your car never needed.

  • Full fault-code read across all systems
  • Live data + road test to confirm the real cause
  • Written report and a fixed quote before any repair

Questions

Diagnostics, answered

What does a car diagnostic actually do?

We plug into your car's onboard computers and read the fault codes they've stored, then interpret them with a road test and live data. You get a plain-English report explaining what's wrong and what it will cost to fix — not just a list of codes.

How much does it cost?

A standard diagnostic scan is £49. If you go ahead with the repair with us, that £49 comes off the bill — so the diagnosis is effectively free.

How long does it take?

A standard scan and report takes about 45 minutes to an hour. Intermittent or complex faults can take longer, and we'll agree that with you before running up any time.

My engine light is on but the car drives fine — should I worry?

It's worth checking. Some causes are minor, but a lit engine light can also mean something that gets more expensive the longer it's left, or an emissions fault that will fail your MOT. A scan tells you which it is.

Do you diagnose electric and hybrid faults?

Yes. Our technicians are IMI-certified for EV and hybrid work and have the tools to read high-voltage and battery system faults safely.
