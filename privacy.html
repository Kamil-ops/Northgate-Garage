Our story

Started in one bay. Same values ever since.

Dave Whitmore opened Northgate in 2011 with a single ramp, a toolbox, and a reputation to build. He'd spent fifteen years at a main dealer and left for one reason: he was tired of watching people get sold work they didn't need.

Today the workshop runs four ramps and a DVSA-approved MOT bay, but the promise hasn't changed. We show you the worn part. We quote before we touch anything. And if it doesn't need doing, we tell you.

Most of our work now comes from customers who've been coming back for a decade, and the neighbours they've sent our way. That's the only marketing an honest garage really needs.

Privacy policy

Last updated: 15 July 2026

This notice explains how Northgate Garage Ltd uses personal information submitted through this website, by telephone or during a garage visit.

Who we are

Northgate Garage Ltd, 12 Northgate Works, Station Road, Watford, WD17 1AB. For privacy questions, email bookings@northgategarage.co.uk or call 01632 960123.

Information we collect

  • Your name, telephone number and email address.
  • Vehicle registration, make, model and service information.
  • Booking preferences, enquiries and records of work completed.
  • Basic website analytics and conversion events, where enabled.

How and why we use it

We use this information to respond to enquiries, manage bookings, provide requested services, maintain service and accounting records, meet legal obligations and protect legitimate business interests. We do not sell personal information.

Who we share it with

Information may be shared where needed with providers of hosting, email, booking, payment or accounting services, and with authorities where legally required. Providers must protect information they process for us.

How long we keep it

Enquiries that do not become bookings are normally removed within 12 months. Booking, invoice and service records may be kept for up to seven years where needed for tax, warranty, safety or legal purposes.

Your rights

Depending on the circumstances, you may ask to access, correct, erase or restrict your information, object to certain uses, or receive a portable copy. You may withdraw consent where consent is the lawful basis.

Complaints and updates

Contact us first so we can try to resolve a concern. You may also complain to the UK Information Commissioner’s Office at ico.org.uk. We may update this notice when our services or duties change.
